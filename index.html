The journey of Baba Saheb Ambedkar

Life, History & Works


Article 1: Birth and Early Life of Dr. B. R. Ambedkar

Bhim Rao Ambedkar was born on 14 April 1891. He was the 14th and youngest child of his parents. His father, Subedar Ramji Maloji Sakpal, served in the British Army and was a follower of Sant Kabir. He was well educated and strongly believed in discipline and learning. When Dr. Ambedkar was just two years old, his father retired from service. At the age of six, he lost his mother. After this tragic loss, his aunt took care of him. He received his early education in Bombay and later studied in Satara. From childhood itself, Babasaheb faced severe discrimination and untouchability. These painful experiences deeply influenced his thoughts and later reforms.


Article 2: Education and Marriage

Despite social hardships, Dr. Ambedkar continued his studies with dedication. He completed his schooling under difficult conditions where he faced discrimination from teachers and classmates. In 1907, after passing matriculation, he got married in a simple ceremony held in a market shed. He completed his graduation from Elphinstone College, Bombay. During this time, he received a scholarship from Maharaja Sayajirao Gaikwad of Baroda, which helped him pursue higher education.


Article 3: Higher Studies Abroad

In 1913, Dr. Ambedkar was selected for higher studies in the United States of America. This became a turning point in his life. He earned his: M.A. degree (1915) Ph.D. degree (1916) from Columbia University. Later, he went to London, where he studied Law at Gray’s Inn and Economics at the London School of Economics. He also studied at Bonn University in Germany. His famous academic works include: Castes in India – Their Mechanism, Genesis and Development Evolution of Provincial Finance in British India Problem of the Rupee – Its Origin and Solution


Article 4: Return to India and Professional Careerd

After returning to India in 1917, Dr. Ambedkar joined the Baroda State service. However, due to caste-based humiliation, he left the job. Later, he worked as a Professor of Political Economy at Sydenham College, Bombay. Because of his deep knowledge, he became very popular among students. In 1928, he joined Government Law College and became its Principal in 1935, serving until 1938.


Article 5: Social Reform and Upliftment of Depressed Classes

In 1924, Dr. Ambedkar founded an organization for the welfare of depressed classes. In 1927, he started the newspaper Bahishkrit Bharat to raise awareness and fight social injustice. In 1935, at a conference in Yeola, he made a historic declaration: “I was born a Hindu, but I will not die a Hindu.” This statement shocked society and inspired millions of oppressed people. In 1936, he formed the Independent Labour Party to protect workers and marginalized communities.


Article 6: Role in Indian Politics and Constitution

In 1942, Dr. Ambedkar was appointed as Labour Member of the Executive Council of the Governor-General. In 1946, he was elected to the Constituent Assembly. After Independence in 1947, he became India’s first Law and Justice Minister in Jawaharlal Nehru’s cabinet and played the most important role in drafting the Indian Constitution. However, in 1951, he resigned due to disagreements over policies and the Hindu Code Bill.
